Waste Management

Generation

Generation refers to the creation of healthcare waste, which includes various materials like used syringes, contaminated dressings, and expired medicines. Hospitals and healthcare facilities generate substantial amounts of waste daily.

Accumulation

Accumulation involves the collection and temporary storage of healthcare waste at healthcare facilities. Proper storage is crucial to prevent contamination and maintain safety.

Handling

Handling encompasses the safe and hygienic management of healthcare waste from its point of generation to storage. It includes the use of personal protective equipment (PPE) and proper waste segregation.

Storage

Storage refers to the secure containment of healthcare waste before its eventual disposal. Different types of healthcare waste may require separate storage facilities to prevent cross-contamination.

Treatment

Treatment involves processes to render healthcare waste less hazardous or non-infectious. It may include methods like autoclaving, incineration, or chemical treatment to reduce the risk associated with the waste.

Cart Heading

Disposal is the final step in healthcare waste management, ensuring that waste is permanently and safely removed from healthcare facilities. This can include landfill disposal, incineration, or other approved methods, depending on regulations and waste types.
